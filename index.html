EIR-P2: Evolving Interaction Representations for Coupled
Prediction and Planning in Autonomous Driving

Chen Shao1, Xuting Duan1,2, Qi Wang1, Jianshan Zhou1, Ling Wang3, Daxin Tian1,2

1School of Transportation Science and Engineering, Beihang University, Beijing, China

2Zhongguancun Laboratory, Beijing, China

3Department of Automation, Tsinghua University, Beijing, China

Abstract

In complex interactive scenarios, ego planning and the future evolution of the scene are tightly coupled over the prediction horizon. Existing methods often generate the full future ego trajectory from the current observation in a single planning pass. This one-shot formulation does not explicitly model the directional dependence between prediction and planning, which limits the ability of the resulting plans to respond to evolving interactions. To address this problem, this paper proposes EIR-P2, a coupled prediction and planning framework. EIR-P2 maintains an interaction representation as a shared intermediate state and updates it through collaborative decoding, enabling the model to capture the dynamic coupling between traffic-agent prediction and ego trajectory planning. The framework first constructs multi-scale hypergraphs to encode group-wise high-order interactions among traffic agents at different spatial granularities. It then uses an interaction-representation-driven collaborative decoder to support multi-round information exchange between traffic-agent prediction and ego planning. Long–Short Horizon Planning Queries are further proposed to represent behavioral tendencies at different temporal scales, generate multimodal ego trajectory candidates, and improve planning adaptability in dynamic interactive scenarios. Experiments on the NuPlan and InterPlan benchmarks show that EIR-P2 achieves better overall performance than existing advanced planning methods and shows strong performance across several representative interactive scenarios, demonstrating its effectiveness and robustness in complex interactive driving environments.

Model Architecture

The main contributions of this paper are summarized as follows:

  • We propose EIR-P2, a coupled prediction and planning framework for interactive driving scenarios. It models the coupling between traffic-agent behavior prediction and ego trajectory planning through a continuously evolving interaction representation, rather than treating the two tasks as independent or weakly related.
  • We design a Multi-scale Hypergraph Interaction Encoding module to characterize group-wise high-order interactions among traffic agents at different spatial granularities. By constructing dynamic hyperedges over multiple agents, this module captures complex multi-agent interaction structures beyond pairwise relations.
  • We introduce an Interaction-Representation-Driven Collaborative Decoder with Long–Short Horizon Planning Queries. The decoder enables multi-round information exchange and collaborative reasoning between prediction and planning, thereby improving the adaptability of ego planning in complex interactive scenarios.
